## 2. Team Goals

- Build a secure and functional enterprise web application that clearly demonstrates the course learning outcomes, especially authentication, authorisation, validation, and privacy-aware design.
- Work professionally as a four-member development team using GitHub, shared planning, documented decisions, and balanced contribution across planning, implementation, testing, and submission.
- Submit a complete assessment package that includes strong technical evidence, clear documentation, and visible contribution from every member in issues, commits, pull requests, and reviews.
- Ensure every member understands the full project, even when responsibilities are divided by module or document section.

---

## 3. Team Procedures

### Communication

- **Teams Group:** Primary channel for day-to-day coordination, reminders, quick blockers, and confirming meeting times.
- **GitHub:** Used for issues, branch work, pull requests, code reviews, commit evidence, and linking work to the rubric.
- **Shared Documents:** Google Docs or Word files used for drafting the requirements document, system design document, testing notes, and final written sections.
- **Meetings / Work Sessions:** A weekly review meeting will be held every Sunday evening (NZ time), with extra sessions scheduled before key deadlines.
- **Progress Updates:** Each member will post a short update after completing a meaningful task or when blocked for more than 24 hours.

### Asynchronous Communication Expectations

- Members are expected to check group messages and GitHub notifications at least once each day during active project weeks.
- Normal response time is within 12 hours on weekdays and within 24 hours on weekends unless prior notice is given.
- If a member will be unavailable because of work, personal commitments, or illness, they must notify the group as early as possible.

### Failure to Communicate

- After 12 hours without a response on an active task, the team leader or assigned facilitator will send a direct reminder.
- After 24 hours without a response, the matter will be raised in the group chat and the task may be reassigned temporarily to protect deadlines.
- After 48 hours without communication and without valid notice, the issue will be documented and escalated to the tutor if it affects project delivery.

## 6. Decision Making and Record Keeping

### Decision Making

- The team will aim for consensus on major choices such as project scope, framework decisions, document content, and final submission quality.
- If consensus cannot be reached within a reasonable time, a majority vote will decide the issue.
- For urgent deadline-driven matters, the team leader may make a temporary decision, but it must be explained to the team and recorded.

### Record Keeping

- Technical work will be recorded in GitHub through issues, commits, pull requests, review comments, and branch history.
- Project decisions and writing progress will be recorded in shared documents and the group communication thread.
- Screenshots of pull requests, discussion threads, commit history, and document version history will be retained as evidence for Task 4 personal contribution summaries.

---

## 7. Team Expectations

### Behaviour Expectations

- Team members will attend scheduled meetings on time or give advance notice if they cannot attend.
- Team members will complete assigned work by the agreed internal deadline, not only by the final due date.
- Team members will review group work respectfully and give constructive suggestions instead of last-minute criticism.
- Team members will not copy work from external sources without acknowledgement and will protect the group from academic integrity issues.
- Team members will communicate blockers early so the team can help instead of waiting until the deadline.

### Participation Strategy

- **Leadership Style:** Shared leadership with Muhammad Moiz Farooq acting as the main coordinator and submission owner.
- **Equal Task Distribution:** Major sections are divided clearly, but each member must still understand the full project and review the others’ work.
- **Including Ideas from All Members:** New ideas are first discussed in the group chat or meeting, then linked to the rubric before adoption.
- **Keeping on Task:** The team will use short internal deadlines, GitHub issues, and a weekly progress check to avoid falling behind.

---

## 8. Dealing with Issues

- Minor disagreements should first be handled respectfully between the involved members.
- If the issue continues, it will be discussed in a full-team meeting and the agreed action will be recorded.
- If a member repeatedly fails to follow agreed procedures, the team may reassign tasks, document missed contributions, and notify the tutor if necessary.
- Repeated non-cooperation may be reflected in peer evaluation forms and personal contribution evidence.
